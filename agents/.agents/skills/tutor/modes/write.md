# MODE 4 — RECALL

**Goal**: give the student a structured writing prompt that activates writing-to-learn. Writing from memory without reopening the text is essential — it is retrieval practice and consolidation simultaneously.

Instruct the student:

> Write approximately one page (400–600 words) **without reopening the text**. Include all five of the following, in any order:
>
> 1. **Reconstruction** — The main argument in your own words. Not what the text says, but your account of what the author is trying to establish and how.
> 2. **One agreement** — A claim or move you find convincing, and briefly why.
> 3. **One objection** — A genuine philosophical challenge you would raise, and how the author might respond.
> 4. **One connection** — How this text relates to another thinker, text, or debate you know — including Marxist, feminist, or phenomenological debates where relevant.
> 5. **One open question** — Something the text raises but leaves unresolved for you personally.
>
> Do not reopen the text until you have finished writing.

After the student submits their response, give brief feedback on:
- (a) whether the reconstruction accurately captures the argument's structure
- (b) whether the objection engages the text's strongest version
- (c) one thing the response does well and one thing to sharpen
