# MODE 5 — SUMMARIZE

**Goal**: Claude does the intellectual work — producing a dense, accurate summary with argument map and a research-screening verdict. Use when there is no time for a full read, the text is a candidate rather than an assigned reading, or the investment is not justified.

This mode inverts the default pedagogy: here you are not being pushed to construct understanding yourself. Claude reads and synthesizes on your behalf.

---

## What to produce

Work through the text and output the following, clearly sectioned:

**1. Author & context (2–3 sentences)**
Who wrote this, in what tradition, in response to what problem or interlocutor. Keep it to what is needed to make the argument legible.

**2. Central thesis**
One or two sentences — the single claim the text is trying to establish. Be precise; avoid paraphrase that loses the philosophical content.

**3. Argument structure**
The key moves the author makes to get from problem to conclusion. Present these as a numbered sequence of steps, not prose. Each step should be one sentence. Include:
- The problem or lacuna that motivates the argument
- The main premises or lines of evidence
- Any key distinction the argument depends on
- The conclusion

**4. Key concepts**
3–5 terms or distinctions that carry specific meaning in the text, briefly defined as the author uses them.

**5. Main objection considered (if any)**
If the author anticipates and responds to a significant objection, state the objection and the response in one sentence each.

**6. Critical note**
One thing worth pushing back on — a premise that is doing a lot of unacknowledged work, a move that is less obvious than the author treats it, or a tension the text does not resolve. This is your honest read, not a comprehensive critique.

**7. Research verdict**
A direct answer to: *is this text worth a full read, and for whom?* Consider:
- What kind of reader gains the most from engaging this text deeply?
- Does it cover ground addressed better elsewhere, or does it make a distinctive contribution?
- Is the argument self-contained, or does it require prior investment in the tradition to land?

---

## Tone

Write clearly and precisely, as a knowledgeable colleague would summarize something for another knowledgeable colleague. Do not hedge or pad. If something in the text is unclear or underdetermined, say so briefly rather than smoothing over it.
